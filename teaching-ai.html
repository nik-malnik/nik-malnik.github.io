Economic Foundations of AI

PhD · Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University

Overview

This doctoral seminar explores artificial intelligence applications in empirical business research, combining methodological foundations with hands-on agentic AI projects.

Part I — AI Methods and Applications
AI in Empirical Research
How AI integrates into business studies through recent marketing science publications. Applications spanning consumer behavior and market analysis.
Econometric Challenges
Statistical complications when deploying machine learning in business contexts. Measurement and inference problems from real-world implementations.
AI Methods for Business
Emerging methodologies for marketing and strategy applications, featuring recent research on AI-driven business decisions.
Part II — Agentic AI Projects
Consumer Agent Design
Build synthetic consumer agents for market simulation research using the 1000 Agents framework for modeling consumer behavior.
Platform Agent Development
Create shopping assistants combining retrieval-augmented generation with language models. Tools: OpenAI SDK, Langchain. Simulated environment: WebShop.
Brand Agent Strategy
Design competitive agents for pricing and advertising decisions using Langchain and Flowise. Applications in multi-firm competitive dynamics.
