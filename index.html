Nikhil Malik

Assistant Professor of Marketing and Business Technology

Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University

My research examines the economics of AI and algorithmic systems — including algorithmic pricing, platform design, and ML feedback loops — using empirical, analytical, and machine learning methods. My work appears in Management Science, Marketing Science, Information Systems Research, and IJRM, and has been featured in CNN, the Boston Globe, and People.

Previously, I was a tenure-track Assistant Professor of Marketing at USC Marshall (2021–2025). Before academia, I was a Vice President and Quantitative Strategist at Goldman Sachs. PhD and MS from CMU; B.Tech from IIT Delhi.
